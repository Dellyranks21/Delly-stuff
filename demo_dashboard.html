Your Real Estate Command Center

Hi — this is the dashboard we wish we'd had when we started. It's built for the modern investor who wants a single command center to see what their portfolio is actually doing: what it's worth today, what it's cash-flowing, where the risks are, and what the next move could unlock. Every number is live. Every slider is interactive. Every scenario — "what if I sell this, refi that, or buy one more door" — is one click away.

How It Works

You're looking at it. Orientation, product story, and the quick-start guide to every interactive feature.

Use when: first visit, or sharing the dashboard with someone new.

⚡ The interactive parts — how to drive it

  • Expense Controls (top of every page) — Four sliders for vacancy, maintenance, CapEx, and reserves. The dashboard defaults to a 5/5/5/5 conservative model. Drag the sliders to see what your portfolio looks like with less conservative (or more paranoid) assumptions.
  • Timeline Projection — Slide "Years Forward" from 0 to 10 to project your portfolio into the future using your rent growth and appreciation assumptions. Every number (value, rent, equity, paydown, freedom %) updates in real time.
  • Click-to-edit values — Hover over any property value, rent, mortgage balance, or rate. See the little pencil? Click it to edit. Changes save automatically and the whole dashboard recalculates.
  • Financial Freedom target — Click your Freedom Target number ($10K/mo by default) to set your personal goal. The progress bars and "properties needed" estimate update to match.
  • BRRR cash-out slider — On the Planning tab, pick a property and drag the LTV slider from 60% to 80%. See the cash you could pull, your new P&I, and what that cash could buy at 5/10/20% down.
  • Deal Simulator — On the Planning tab, model a hypothetical next purchase. Price, rent, down %, rate, units — every field is a slider. Toggle "Include in portfolio totals" to see what adding that door does to your freedom progress.
  • Stress Test — On the Safety tab, see your cash flow under five preset scenarios (rate shock, vacancy, maintenance, market drop, full recession). Each scenario is rebuilt from your live numbers. Build your own: the form at the bottom lets you create custom what-ifs — name it, plug in rent drop / added vacancy / added maintenance / rate shock / non-payment-or-eviction rate, and it shows up alongside the presets with a "Custom" tag. Saved to your browser; delete anytime.
  • Share button (top right) — Exports a snapshot HTML file with your current numbers baked in. Send it to a lender, partner, coach, or save it as a point-in-time archive.
  • Persistence — Everything you type, every slider you set, every tab you pick is saved locally in your browser. Close the tab, come back tomorrow, your state is exactly where you left it.

🆕 New in v4.5 — Build Your Own Stress Scenarios

The dashboard ships with five preset stress scenarios (Base, Mild Stress, Rate Shock, 2008 Repeat, Worst Case) — those are the universal benchmarks every investor should know. But every portfolio is different, and one piece of community feedback was clear: "I want to model the things I'm actually worried about."

So on the Safety tab → Stress Test → "🧪 Build Your Own Scenario" there's now a builder where you define your own what-if. Six knobs:

  • Rent Change % — what if asking rents drop 8% in your market?
  • Added Vacancy % — extra turnover above your slider baseline.
  • Added Maintenance % — a bad repair year on top of normal.
  • Rate Shock (pp) — what your P&I looks like if you had to refinance today at +1.5pp.
  • Non-Payment / Eviction % — the rent you bill but never collect.
  • Value Change % — for the equity-impact view.

Hit + Add Custom Scenario and it appears in the grid right next to the presets with a "Custom" tag, color-coded by severity (green if it's mild, gold if it bites, red if it hurts). Each card shows monthly cash flow, the delta vs. base, and the equity position. Hover the × to delete. Everything is saved to your browser — refresh, close the tab, come back next week, your scenarios are still there.

Math note: non-payment is modeled as a reduction to collected rent, while variable expenses still scale with gross rent — a deliberately conservative assumption since your bills don't disappear when a tenant stops paying.

Transparency · Methodology

🔬 The Math

The question every investor should ask: how do I know these numbers are right? The answer — because the formulas are industry-standard (what a CPA, lender, or underwriter would use), every input is yours to verify, the methodology is documented, and the dashboard runs a self-check on every page load. No black box. The value here is the presentation, the liveness, and the scenario modeling — not the math itself.

📐 The core formulas

💵 Net Cash Flow (per property)
CF = Rent − PITI − Utilities − Water − Lawn − TaxPrep − (Rent × [Vac+Mnt+CpX+Res])
Monthly rent collected, minus the full PITI mortgage payment (P&I + taxes + insurance + PMI), minus the fixed operating costs you've entered, minus variable operating reserves computed as a % of gross rent (your four sliders).
📊 Cap Rate
Cap Rate = (Annual NOI ÷ Current Value) × 100
NOI = annual rent minus operating expenses excluding debt service. Cap rate lets you compare properties independent of financing. A 6% cap in CT is roughly what we target for solid B-class rentals.
🏦 DSCR (Debt Service Coverage)
DSCR = Annual NOI ÷ Annual P&I Debt Service
The ratio lenders use. Above 1.20 is healthy (20% cushion). Below 1.0 means the property doesn't cover its mortgage from operations. Banks won't finance DSCR < 1.0 on investment loans.
💰 Cash-on-Cash Return
CoC = (Annual Net CF ÷ Cash Invested) × 100
Return on the actual dollars you put in (down payment + closing costs + rehab). Ignores equity gains and principal paydown. 8-12% CoC is a decent target for buy-and-hold.
🎯 Financial Freedom %
Freedom % = (Total Monthly Net CF ÷ Goal) × 100
Sum of net cash flow across all properties, divided by your monthly target (defaults to $10K, click to edit). This is the North Star — if it hits 100%, your rentals cover your lifestyle.
📅 Loan Amortization (Payoff Year)
n = −ln(1 − B·r/P) ÷ ln(1+r)
Standard amortization math solved for n (months remaining), given current balance B, monthly rate r, and monthly P&I payment P. Extra principal payments shorten n; the table calculates interest saved vs. the original schedule.
⚠️ Stress Test Scenarios
Stress CF = (Rent × RentMult × (1−NonPay)) − PITI' − Ops'
Rent is multiplied by your rent-change factor and reduced by any non-payment rate. If rate shock applies, P&I is recomputed at the new rate on the remaining term. Variable expenses still scale with gross rent (conservative — bills don't disappear when tenants don't pay).
📈 Equity & Paydown
Equity = Current Value − Loan Balance
Monthly Paydown = P&I − (Balance × Rate ÷ 12)
Equity is straightforward. Monthly principal paydown is the part of your P&I payment that's not interest — that's the portion reducing your loan balance, aka forced savings.

🏛️ The four pillars of trust

📥 Inputs are yours
Values, rents, balances, rates, purchase prices — every number flows from what you entered (click-to-edit any field). Garbage in → garbage out, but it's your garbage and you can change it in 2 seconds.
🌐 External data is public
The LIVE mortgage rate bar pulls from Freddie Mac's Primary Mortgage Market Survey (published weekly, publicly available). No private APIs, no scraped sources, no black boxes.
✅ Self-check on every load
Every page load runs a health check: all scripts parse, every tab has its sections, version stamp reconciles. Results appear in the footer version line ("✅ all systems go" or "⚠️ N issues"). If something breaks, you see it immediately.
📝 Audit trail
Every version change is logged in a formula audit document (dated markdown file). Every new calculation or modification is documented with what, why, and the math identity check. You can compare any version to any other.
